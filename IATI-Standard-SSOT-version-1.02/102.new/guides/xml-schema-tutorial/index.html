Background

This section provides general background information about IATI documents.

IATI document types

IATI has defined two XML document types:

Â 

  1. The Organizations document, describing a single organization (such as a donor, foundation, agency, or NGO) involved in aid work. Â This document may include aggregate budget information for the organization.
  2. The Activities document, describing a single aid activity (such as a project or humanitarian appeal). This document may include specific budget information for the activity, including inflows and outflows of money.

Â 

For example, letâs say that in 2011 the fictitious ACME Aid Agency were funding three projects, divided evenly between Rwanda and Burundi, each worth â¬500,000:

  1. The organization document would state that ACME has budgeted a total of â¬3,000,000 for aid in 2011, and would further specify that â¬1,500,000 was allocated for activities in Rwanda and â¬1,500,000 was allocated for activities in Burundi.
  2. The three activity documents would each state that the total budget of the activity was â¬1,000,000, of which â¬500,000 was allocated for Rwanda, and â¬500,000 allocated for Burundi.

Â 

The formal definitions of these document types appear as two XML schemas, iati-organizations-schema.xsd and iati-activities-schema.xsd. Â It is important to understand, however, that these schemas do not provide a complete definition of the IATI document types: it is possible for a document to validate against one of the schemas and still not be a conformant IATI aid document.

Â 

Since the activities document type is the most widely used (and the best defined at this early stage of IATI development), this tutorial will concentrate solely on it; however, many of the points made in this tutorial also apply to the organization document type.

Adding other information

There will be situations where reporting organizations want to publish additional data that are not covered by the default IATI elements, or want to specify the data using the original formats or values to avoid information loss. Â IATI provides two mechanisms for doing so:
